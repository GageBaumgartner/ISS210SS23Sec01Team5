About

The differences in education in first versus third world countries allows for unequal and unfair education opportunities for children across the world. (Education should be more regulated everywhere so that people–regardless of class or status–have the ability to learn and grow). This is important to us, as we are students at Michigan State University, we don't know the full story of the unfair educational opportunities in third world countries. So, why does providing fair educational opportunities matter? Well, we know that educational disparities are correlated with skin color, ethnicity, linguistic and social class status. And overtime inequality of denial of equal access and opportunities have been compounding, having adverse effects on lower status groups. Without intervention the opportunity gap will increase as it has, making it harder for countries and groups of people to be successful in their educational achievement. Luckily there are always ways to help out. Somethings that can help lead to fair educational opportunities are by increasing equality among first and third world countries, increasing participation, increasing social networks, increasing access to credit to overcome cost barriers, and increasing the motivation from teachers and students alike.

Online Schooling

Online schooling can be a promising solution for bringing education to 3rd world countries. With internet access, students in remote areas can receive quality education from anywhere in the world. Online learning platforms can provide access to a wide range of educational resources, including interactive content, video lectures, and digital textbooks. Moreover, online schooling can help overcome challenges such as lack of infrastructure, funding, and teacher shortages. Overall, online schooling has the potential to provide equal educational opportunities to students in 3rd world countries.( talks about how online schooling is what will be the savior for those 3rd world country schools.
