Unequal Education Opportunities for Children

Education Inequality Leaves Children in Third World Countries with a Lesser Quality of Life

Portfolio

About

The differences in education in first versus third world countries allows for unequal and unfair education opportunities for children across the world. This is important to us, as we are students at Michigan State University; we do not know about the full stories of those struggling to obtain a basic education in developing nations, but we can use our voices to call for change.

So, why does providing fair educational opportunities matter? Well, we know that educational disparities are correlated with skin color, ethnicity, linguistic and social class status. And over time, inequality of denial of equivalent access and opportunities have been compounding, having adverse effects on lower status groups. Without intervention, the gap in the quality of schooling will increase as it has been, making it more difficult for groups of people (and their countries as a whole) to be successful in their educational achievement.

Luckily, there are always ways to help out; some things that can help lead to fair schooling opportunities are increasing equality among first and third world countries, participation, social networks, access to credit to overcome cost barriers, and increasing the motivation from teachers and students alike.

Education should be more regulated everywhere so that people–regardless of class or status–have identical abilities/chances to learn and grow.

Pakistan: Improving Education through Greater Accountability and Resources in Punjab Province

Content Warning: Minor Flashing of Lighting Between Scenes (Epilepsy Warning)

This five-minute video discusses the efforts made by the government of Punjab, a province in Pakistan, to improve education through greater accountability and resources. The government has launched various initiatives such as the School Performance Management System, which tracks the performance of schools and teachers, and the Punjab Education Foundation, which provides funding and resources to low-cost private schools. The government has also increased funding for education and introduced reforms to improve teacher training and curriculum development.

These efforts have resulted in improvements in student enrollment, attendance, and learning outcomes. One teacher’s words about the schooling techniques particularly stands out–she says, “We were taught about activity-based learning, and how to teach so that children learn properly while enjoying. It is a big difference.” These types of efforts are vital to making education an equal experience around the world, as it is a common occurrence that once schooling is brought to developing regions, its quality still remains poor and therefore children do not receive the education they need and deserve.

Online Schooling

Online schooling can be a promising solution for bringing education to 3rd world countries. With internet access, students in remote areas can receive quality education from anywhere in the world. Online learning platforms can provide access to a wide range of educational resources, including interactive content, video lectures, and digital textbooks. Moreover, online schooling can help overcome challenges such as lack of infrastructure, funding, and teacher shortages. Overall, online schooling has the potential to provide equal educational opportunities to students in 3rd world countries.

Importance of equal education around the world

Access to school is affected by a variety of factors, including cultural background, gender, family circumstances, and so on, but one of the most significant factors influencing access to school is money. The disparities between the richest and poorest people, as well as those living in rural and urban regions, have not diminished. 17% of the world's children, adolescents, and youth, are not in school. The proportion is substantially higher in developing countries: 31% in Sub-Saharan Africa and 21% in Central Asia, compared to 3% in Europe and North America. Because education has been shown to impact general well-being, productivity, social capital, responsible citizenship, and sustainable behavior, the severity of the situation in third-world nations can be substantial. Its equal distribution enables the development of permeable societies and equity.
